## 5. Curriculum

**5 weeks. 10 sessions. 5 real artifacts.**

### Week 1 — From prompt to first agent

In the first session you set up your personal agent and put it to work on a real task. In the second you learn to instruct it like a professional: prompts, context, and the art of directing intelligence.

**What you learn:** What separates an agent from a chatbot. How to provision an agent in Studio. Prompt structure, context layers, system instructions.

**What you build:** A personal agent active in Studio, solving a real task from your actual work.

---

### Week 2 — Agent architecture

We open the engine. You understand how bootstrap files, memory, context, and skills work — the pieces that separate a real agent from a chatbot in disguise.

**What you learn:** Bootstrap files, persistent vs. ephemeral memory, context layers, skills as composable capabilities.

**What you build:** An agent with its own architecture, configured memory, and functional skills you created yourself.

---

### Week 3 — Integrations, tools, and identity

Your agent leaves the sandbox. It connects to your email, calendar, files, your stack — and activates its own persistent identity. From this moment, everything it does is recorded in its track record.

**What you learn:** Tool calling, native integrations (Gmail, Calendar, GitHub, files), what it means for an agent to have its own identity, how reputation starts building automatically.

**What you build:** An agent connected to your real tools, with its own identity, inside an operational workspace. Track record starts accumulating.

---

### Week 4 — Workflows and agent economics

You design workflows that run without you. You define how your agent charges for its outputs or pays for services — Studio handles the rails. From isolated tasks to complete processes that transact.

**What you learn:** Workflow design (triggers, multi-step, conditionals, error recovery, human-in-the-loop), how to declare payment rules, when an agent should pay vs. charge, spend controls.

**What you build:** A multi-step workflow solving a real case, with a working economic layer — your agent pays, charges, or both.

---

### Week 5 — Iteration, reputation, and Demo Day

You review the track record your agent has been building since Week 3. You iterate with real evidence. We close with Demo Day: you present your capstone in front of sponsors, investors, and the community.

**What you learn:** Basic evals, logging, tracing, common failure patterns, evidence-based iteration, how to read and present your agent's verified track record.

**What you build:** A capstone with recorded demo, real metrics, documented case, and a verified track record of your agent's work.
